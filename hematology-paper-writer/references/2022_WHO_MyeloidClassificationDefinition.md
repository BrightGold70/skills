### I. Clonal Haematopoiesis

Clonal haematopoiesis (CH) is formally recognized as a precursor myeloid disease state defined by the presence of a mutated multipotent stem/progenitor cell harbouring a selective growth advantage 1, 2\.  
Disease Category / Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
Clonal haematopoiesis of indeterminate potential (CHIP),Somatic mutations of myeloid malignancy-associated genes detected in blood or bone marrow at a variant allele fraction (VAF) $\\ge 2\\%$ ($\\ge 4\\%$ for X-linked gene mutations in males) without a diagnosed haematologic disorder or unexplained cytopenia 3\.  
Clonal cytopenia of undetermined significance (CCUS),"CHIP detected in the presence of one or more persistent cytopenias otherwise unexplained by haematologic or non-haematologic conditions, not meeting criteria for defined myeloid neoplasms 4."

### II. Myeloproliferative Neoplasms (MPN)

The MPN category relies on integrating molecular data, peripheral blood findings, and bone marrow morphology 5, 6\.  
Disease Category / Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
Chronic myeloid leukaemia (CML),"Defined by the BCR::ABL1 fusion 7\. Divided into Chronic Phase and Blast Phase ($\\ge 20\\%$ myeloid blasts, extramedullary proliferation, or increased lymphoblasts); the Accelerated Phase is no longer recognized 7, 8."  
Polycythaemia vera (PV),"Elevated haemoglobin and/or haematocrit, trilineage hyperplasia (panmyelosis) with pleomorphic mature megakaryocytes, and JAK2 V617F or exon 12 mutations 6, 9."  
Essential thrombocythaemia (ET),"Sustained thrombocytosis, proliferation of enlarged mature megakaryocytes with hyperlobulated nuclei, and driver mutations (JAK2, CALR, MPL) 9, 10."  
Primary myelofibrosis (PMF),"Proliferation of abnormal megakaryocytes and granulocytes with secondary reticulin/collagen marrow fibrosis 10\. Subdivided into prefibrotic PMF and fibrotic PMF 7, 10."  
Chronic neutrophilic leukaemia (CNL),"WBC $\\ge 25 \\times 10^9$/L with $\\ge 80\\%$ segmented neutrophils and bands, bone marrow hypercellularity, and frequently CSF3R mutations 11."  
Chronic eosinophilic leukaemia (CEL),"Sustained hypereosinophilia for 4 weeks with clonality and abnormal bone marrow morphology, lacking specific TK fusions 11, 12\. The ""NOS"" qualifier is omitted 12."  
Juvenile myelomonocytic leukaemia (JMML),"Haematopoietic stem cell-derived MPN of early childhood driven by unchecked activation of the RAS pathway, lacking KMT2A fusions 13."  
"MPN, not otherwise specified (MPN-NOS)","Clinical, laboratory, and molecular features of MPN lacking criteria of any specific MPN type or with overlapping features 12, 13."

### III. Mastocytosis

Characterized by an accumulation of abnormal mast cells, typically driven by constitutive activation of the *KIT* receptor 14\.  
Disease Category / Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
Cutaneous mastocytosis (CM),"Clonal mast cell disease confined to the skin (includes Urticaria pigmentosa, diffuse CM, and cutaneous mastocytoma) 15."  
Systemic mastocytosis (SM),"Multifocal dense infiltrates of atypical mast cells in extracutaneous organs 14\. Subtypes include Bone marrow mastocytosis (new), Indolent SM, Smoldering SM, Aggressive SM, and SM with an associated haematologic neoplasm 15, 16\. KIT mutations or CD30 expression serve as minor criteria 16."  
Mast cell leukaemia,A highly aggressive subtype of systemic mastocytosis with significant bone marrow infiltration by atypical mast cells 15\.  
Mast cell sarcoma,"Localized destructive growth of highly atypical neoplastic mast cells 14, 15."

### IV. Myelodysplastic Neoplasms (MDS)

MDS classification is now genetically grouped, completely replacing "syndromes" with "neoplasms" and setting the dysplasia threshold at 10% for all lineages 17\.  
Disease Category / Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
MDS with low blasts and isolated 5q deletion (MDS-5q),$\<5\\%$ BM and $\<2\\%$ PB blasts; 5q deletion alone or with 1 other abnormality (excluding monosomy 7/7q- or complex karyotype) 18\.  
MDS with low blasts and SF3B1 mutation (MDS-SF3B1),"$\<5\\%$ BM blasts, SF3B1 mutation, lacking 5q deletion, monosomy 7, or complex karyotype 18\. $\\ge 15\\%$ ring sideroblasts can substitute for SF3B1 mutation 19."  
MDS with biallelic TP53 inactivation (MDS-biTP53),"$\<20\\%$ blasts with usually complex karyotype and two or more TP53 mutations, or one mutation with concurrent copy loss/LOH 18."  
MDS with low blasts (MDS-LB),Morphologically defined; $\<5\\%$ BM and $\<2\\%$ PB blasts 19\.  
"MDS, hypoplastic (MDS-h)","Morphologically defined; $\\le 25\\%$ bone marrow cellularity (age-adjusted) with cytopenias and dysplasia 19, 20."  
MDS with increased blasts (MDS-IB),"Subdivided into MDS-IB1 (5–9% BM or 2–4% PB blasts) and MDS-IB2 (10–19% BM or 5–19% PB blasts, or Auer rods) 19."  
MDS with fibrosis (MDS-f),5–19% BM; 2–19% PB blasts associated with marrow fibrosis 19\.  
Childhood MDS with low blasts,Replaces refractory cytopenia of childhood (RCC); includes hypocellular and NOS subtypes 21-23.  
Childhood MDS with increased blasts,5–19% BM blasts or 2–19% PB blasts in children 23\.

### V. Myelodysplastic/Myeloproliferative Neoplasms (MDS/MPN)

Neoplasms defined by overlapping pathologic and molecular features of MDS and MPN 24\.  
Disease Category / Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
Chronic myelomonocytic leukaemia (CMML),"Persistent absolute ($\\ge 0.5 \\times 10^9$/L) and relative ($\\ge 10\\%$) monocytosis, $\<20\\%$ blasts 25, 26\. Subtyped by WBC into MD-CMML ($\<13 \\times 10^9$/L) and MP-CMML ($\\ge 13 \\times 10^9$/L) 27\. Subgrouped into CMML-1 and CMML-2 based on blasts 28."  
MDS/MPN with neutrophilia,"Replaces atypical CML; features prominent neutrophilia and severe dysgranulopoiesis 23, 29."  
MDS/MPN with SF3B1 mutation and thrombocytosis,"Redefined primarily by the presence of an SF3B1 mutation accompanying concurrent thrombocytosis and myelodysplasia 23, 29."  
"MDS/MPN, not otherwise specified","Overlapping MDS/MPN features failing to meet diagnostic criteria of specific types 23, 29."

### VI. Acute Myeloid Leukaemia (AML)

The WHO 2022 classification eliminates the 20% blast requirement for most AML types with defining genetic abnormalities, separating them from morphology-defined AML 30, 31\.  
Disease Category / Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
APL with PML::RARA fusion,"Acute promyelocytic leukaemia driven by t(15;17)(q24.1;q21.2) / PML::RARA 32, 33."  
AML with RUNX1::RUNX1T1 fusion,"Driven by t(8;21)(q22;q22.1) 32, 33."  
AML with CBFB::MYH11 fusion,"Driven by inv(16)(p13.1q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22) 32, 33."  
AML with DEK::NUP214 fusion,"Driven by t(6;9)(p22.3;q34.1) 32, 33."  
AML with RBM15::MRTFA fusion,"Formerly RBM15::MKL1; specific rare gene fusion 32, 33."  
AML with BCR::ABL1 fusion,"Driven by t(9;22)(q34.1;q11.2); requires $\\ge 20\\%$ blasts to distinguish from CML 33, 34."  
AML with KMT2A rearrangement,"Fusions involving 11q23.3 (e.g., MLLT3, AFDN, ELL, MLLT10); no minimum blast threshold 33, 35."  
AML with MECOM rearrangement,"Rearrangements involving 3q26.2; no minimum blast threshold 33, 35."  
AML with NUP98 rearrangement,"Rearrangements involving 11p15.4; no minimum blast threshold 33, 35."  
AML with NPM1 mutation,"Somatic mutations of NPM1; no minimum blast threshold 31, 35."  
AML with CEBPA mutation,"Biallelic or single basic leucine zipper (smbZIP) mutations; requires $\\ge 20\\%$ blasts 31, 36."  
"AML, myelodysplasia-related (AML-MR)","$\\ge 20\\%$ blasts with specific cytogenetics (e.g., complex, 5q/7q deletions), 8 specific MDS-related mutations (ASXL1, BCOR, EZH2, SF3B1, SRSF2, STAG2, U2AF1, ZRSR2), or history of MDS/MPN 37-40."  
AML with other defined genetic alterations,"Emerging rare fusions and genetic drivers 37, 38, 41."  
AML defined by differentiation,"Cases lacking genetic defining abnormalities requiring $\\ge 20\\%$ blasts. Includes: AML with minimal differentiation, AML without maturation, AML with maturation, Acute basophilic, Acute myelomonocytic, Acute monocytic, and Acute megakaryoblastic leukaemia 38, 42-45."  
Acute erythroid leukaemia (AEL),"Neoplastic proliferation of erythroid cells ($\\ge 80\\%$ of cellularity) with $\\ge 30\\%$ proerythroblasts, often featuring biallelic TP53 alterations; blast threshold modified/exempt 44, 46."  
Myeloid sarcoma,"Unique tissue-based extramedullary manifestation of AML, maintaining derivation from a common haematopoietic precursor 47."

### VII. Secondary Myeloid Neoplasms

A segregated category for myeloid neoplasms driven by known cytotoxic exposures or germline alterations 48\.  
Disease Category / Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
Myeloid neoplasms post cytotoxic therapy (MN-pCT),"AML, MDS, or MDS/MPN arising after DNA-damaging therapy or PARP1 inhibitors 49, 50\. Must append qualifier to primary phenotype (e.g., AML with KMT2A rearrangement post cytotoxic therapy) 40."  
Myeloid neoplasms associated with germline predisposition,"Integrated formulaic qualifier appended to the clinical phenotype. Categories include: 1\) Without pre-existing platelet disorder (CEBPA, DDX41, TP53); 2\) With pre-existing platelet disorder (RUNX1, ANKRD26, ETV6); 3\) With potential organ dysfunction (GATA2, Bone marrow failure syndromes, RASopathies, Down syndrome, SAMD9/SAMD9L) 51-53."

### VIII. Myeloid/Lymphoid Neoplasms with Eosinophilia and TK Gene Fusions (MLN-TK)

Neoplasms driven by specific kinase rearrangements leading to constitutive activation, superseding most MDS, MPN, and AML diagnoses 54\.  
Disease Category / Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
MLN with PDGFRA rearrangement,Typically cryptic 4q12 deletion (FIP1L1::PDGFRA) 54-56.  
MLN with PDGFRB rearrangement,"Most commonly t(5;12)(q32;p13.2) / ETV6::PDGFRB 55, 56."  
MLN with FGFR1 rearrangement,"Aberration of chromosome 8p11 55, 56."  
MLN with JAK2 rearrangement,"Includes PCM1::JAK2 and other partners 55, 56."  
MLN with FLT3 rearrangement,"Rearrangements involving chromosome 13q12.2 55, 56."  
MLN with ETV6::ABL1 fusion,"Distinct from B-ALL with ETV6::ABL1, responsive to TKIs 55, 56."  
MLN with other defined TK fusions,"Rare kinase fusions (e.g., ETV6::FGFR2, ETV6::LYN, ETV6::NTRK3) 56, 57."

### IX. Acute Leukaemias of Ambiguous Lineage (ALAL) / MPAL

Grouped under a unified category based on overlapping immunophenotypic and molecular pathogenic mechanisms 58, 59\.  
Disease Category / Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
MPAL with BCR::ABL1 fusion,Mixed-phenotype leukaemia with t(9;22)(q34.1;q11.2) 60\.  
MPAL with KMT2A rearrangement,Mixed-phenotype leukaemia involving 11q23.3 translocations 60\.  
MPAL with ZNF384 rearrangement,"Fusions with TCF3, EP300, TAF15, CREBBP; common B/myeloid phenotype 59, 60."  
ALAL with BCL11B rearrangement,"Associated with acute undifferentiated leukaemia or T/myeloid MPAL 59, 60."  
ALAL with other defined genetic alterations,New landing category for rare emerging ALAL genetic subtypes 60\.  
"ALAL, immunophenotypically defined","Cases lacking genetic driver, assigned by flow cytometry intensity/pattern. Includes: MPAL B/myeloid, MPAL T/myeloid, MPAL rare types, ALAL not otherwise specified, and Acute undifferentiated leukaemia (AUL) 60-62."

### X. Histiocytic/Dendritic Cell Neoplasms

Clonal proliferations placed after myeloid neoplasms to acknowledge their shared derivation from common monocytic/histiocytic/dendritic myeloid progenitors 63, 64\.  
Disease Category / Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
Mature plasmacytoid dendritic cell proliferation,"Clonal MPDCP cells associated with a concurrent myeloid neoplasm (CMML, AML, MDS) 65, 66."  
Blastic plasmacytoid dendritic cell neoplasm,"Aggressive pDC neoplasm defined by strict immunophenotypic criteria (CD123+, CD4+, CD56+, TCF4+, TCL1+) 65, 67."  
Langerhans cell neoplasms,"Includes Langerhans cell histiocytosis and Langerhans cell sarcoma 68, 69."  
Other dendritic cell neoplasms,"Includes Indeterminate dendritic cell tumour and Interdigitating dendritic cell sarcoma 64, 69."  
Histiocytic neoplasms,"Phenotypically diverse but genetically linked via MAPK pathway mutations. Includes: Juvenile xanthogranuloma, Erdheim-Chester disease, Rosai-Dorfman disease, ALK-positive histiocytosis, and Histiocytic sarcoma 68-70."
