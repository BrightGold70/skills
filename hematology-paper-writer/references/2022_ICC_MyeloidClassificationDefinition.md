### I. Myeloproliferative Neoplasms (MPNs)

The 2022 ICC refines MPN diagnostic criteria by rigorously integrating molecular data (e.g., *JAK2, CALR, MPL, CSF3R* mutations) with strictly defined morphologic features, specifically emphasizing bone marrow fibrosis grading and megakaryocytic atypia 1-4.  
Disease Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
"Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML), Chronic Phase","Driven by the BCR::ABL1 fusion 1, 5."  
"CML, Accelerated Phase (AP)","Defined by 10%–19% blasts in bone marrow (BM) or peripheral blood (PB), $ \\ge 20\\% $ PB basophils, or the presence of an additional clonal cytogenetic abnormality (ACA) in Ph+ cells 6, 7."  
"CML, Blast Phase (BP)","Defined by $ \\ge 20\\% $ blasts in BM/PB or the presence of an extramedullary myeloid sarcoma; increasing lymphoblasts ($\>5\\%$) may indicate impending lymphoid BP 6, 7."  
Polycythemia Vera (PV),"Requires elevated hemoglobin (\>16.5 g/dL men; \>16.0 g/dL women) or hematocrit, presence of JAK2 V617F or exon 12 mutation, and BM panmyelosis with pleomorphic, mature megakaryocytes 8, 9."  
Post-PV Myelofibrosis,"Progression from established PV, strictly requiring BM reticulin/collagen fibrosis of grade 2 or 3, along with anemia, leukoerythroblastosis, increasing splenomegaly, or constitutional symptoms 8, 10."  
Essential Thrombocythemia (ET),"Requires platelet count $ \\ge 450 \\times 10^9/L $, proliferation of enlarged, mature megakaryocytes with hyperlobulated nuclei, absence of significant BM fibrosis, and a JAK2, CALR, or MPL mutation 11, 12."  
Post-ET Myelofibrosis,"Progression from established ET, strictly requiring BM fibrosis of grade 2 or 3, accompanied by anemia ($\>2$ g/dL drop), leukoerythroblastosis, or increasing splenomegaly 12, 13."  
Early/Prefibrotic Primary Myelofibrosis (pre-PMF),"BM showing megakaryocytic proliferation/atypia with dense clusters, granulocytic proliferation, grade \< 2 fibrosis, and an MPN-associated driver mutation (JAK2, CALR, MPL) or other clonal marker 14, 15."  
Overt Primary Myelofibrosis (PMF),"Requires megakaryocytic atypia accompanied by reticulin and/or collagen fibrosis grade 2 or 3, plus an MPN driver mutation, along with minor clinical criteria (anemia, leukocytosis, elevated LDH, splenomegaly) 15, 16."  
Chronic Neutrophilic Leukemia (CNL),"Defined by a WBC $ \\ge 13 \\times 10^9/L $ with $ \\ge 80\\% $ segmented/band neutrophils, $ \< 10\\% $ neutrophil precursors, $ \< 10\\% $ monocytes, hypercellular BM, and an activating CSF3R mutation (e.g., T618I) 17, 18."  
"Chronic Eosinophilic Leukemia, NOS (CEL, NOS)","Persistent hypereosinophilia ($ \\ge 1.5 \\times 10^9/L $ and $ \\ge 10\\% $ WBC), blasts \< 20%, dysplastic megakaryocytes and/or significant BM fibrosis, and demonstration of a clonal mutation, lacking specific TK fusions 19, 20."  
"MPN, Unclassifiable (MPN-U)","Clinical and hematologic features of MPN with a JAK2, CALR, or MPL mutation, but failing to meet diagnostic thresholds for PV, ET, or PMF; typically captures early-phase disease or MPN with concurrent inflammatory masking 21-23."

### II. Myeloid/Lymphoid Neoplasms with Eosinophilia and Tyrosine Kinase (TK) Gene Fusions

This category emphasizes kinase-driven rearrangements presenting as chronic myeloid neoplasms (often mimicking CEL or CMML) or acute leukemias, necessitating specific targeted therapies (TKIs) 24, 25\.  
Disease Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
Neoplasm with PDGFRA rearrangement,"Typically features cryptic 4q12 deletion (FIP1L1::PDGFRA), manifesting as CEL-like BM with frequent extramedullary involvement; highly responsive to TKIs 5, 26."  
Neoplasm with PDGFRB rearrangement,"Often ETV6::PDGFRB t(5;12)(q32;p13.2), presenting with CEL-like features or monocytosis with eosinophilia 5, 26."  
Neoplasm with FGFR1 rearrangement,"Often ZMYM2::FGFR1 t(8;13), typically presenting as extramedullary T-ALL/lymphoma with concurrent MPN-like BM 2, 27."  
Neoplasm with JAK2 rearrangement,"Most commonly PCM1::JAK2 t(8;9), presenting as an MPN or MDS/MPN-like BM with eosinophilia and distinct erythroid microtumors 2, 27."  
Neoplasm with FLT3 rearrangement,"Most commonly ETV6::FLT3 t(12;13), frequently manifesting as T-ALL or myeloid sarcoma with an MPN-like BM 2, 28."  
Neoplasm with ETV6::ABL1,"CML-like presentation with frequent eosinophilia in chronic or blast phase; responsive to second-generation TKIs 2, 28."

### III. Mastocytosis

Mastocytosis is defined by the clonal infiltration of atypical mast cells, universally evaluating for the *KIT* D816V mutation and tryptase expression 23, 29\.  
Disease Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
Systemic Mastocytosis (SM),"Requires multifocal dense infiltrates of tryptase/CD117+ mast cells ($ \\ge 15 $ cells/aggregate). Minor criteria include atypical spindle morphology, aberrant CD25/CD2/CD30 expression, KIT D816V mutation, and persistently elevated serum tryptase \>20 ng/mL 30, 31\. Subtypes include Indolent, Smoldering, and Aggressive SM 29."  
Systemic Mastocytosis with an Associated Myeloid Neoplasm (SM-AMN),"SM coexisting with another fully classified myeloid neoplasm (e.g., CMML, AML, MDS). The KIT mutation is often shared between the two neoplastic compartments 32, 33."  
Mast Cell Leukemia,Aggressive variant where atypical immature mast cells account for $ \\ge 20\\% $ of the bone marrow cellularity 25\.  
Cutaneous Mastocytosis,"Clonal mast cell disease almost always confined to the skin, typically observed in pediatric populations 2, 29."

### IV. Myelodysplastic/Myeloproliferative Neoplasms (MDS/MPN)

These neoplasms require the strict concurrent presence of cytopenias and myeloproliferative features (cytosis), relying heavily on molecular clonality in the absence of defining cytogenetics 34, 35\.  
Disease Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
Chronic Myelomonocytic Leukemia (CMML),"Requires monocytes $ \\ge 0.5 \\times 10^9/L $ and $ \\ge 10\\% $ of WBC, cytopenia, blasts \< 20%, and evidence of clonality (e.g., TET2, ASXL1, SRSF2 $ \\ge 10\\% $ VAF). Subdivided into CMML-1 (\<5% PB blasts) and CMML-2 (5-19% PB blasts) 36-38."  
Clonal Monocytosis of Undetermined Significance (CMUS),Persistent monocytosis ($ \\ge 0.5 \\times 10^9/L $ and $ \\ge 10\\% $ WBC) with a myeloid neoplasm-associated mutation ($ \\ge 2\\% $ VAF) but completely lacking cytopenia or BM morphologic features of CMML 39-41.  
Clonal Cytopenia with Monocytosis of Undetermined Significance (CCMUS),"Meets CMUS criteria but presents with cytopenia, marking a higher-risk precursor state to overt MDS/MPN 39, 41."  
Atypical Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (aCML),"Leukocytosis $ \\ge 13 \\times 10^9/L $, $ \\ge 10\\% $ neutrophil precursors, profound dysgranulopoiesis, monocytes \< 10%, no eosinophilia, and absence of BCR::ABL1 or MPN driver mutations; strongly supported by SETBP1 and ASXL1 comutations 42-45."  
MDS/MPN with thrombocytosis and SF3B1 mutation (MDS/MPN-T-SF3B1),"Platelet count $ \\ge 450 \\times 10^9/L $, anemia, blasts \< 5% in BM, and SF3B1 mutation (VAF \> 10%). Finding ring sideroblasts is common but no longer obligatory 46, 47."  
"MDS/MPN with ring sideroblasts and thrombocytosis, NOS (MDS/MPN-RS-T, NOS)","Platelet count $ \\ge 450 \\times 10^9/L $, anemia, $ \\ge 15\\% $ ring sideroblasts, and demonstration of a clonal mutation, but explicitly lacking the SF3B1 mutation 48-50."  
"MDS/MPN, Not Otherwise Specified (MDS/MPN, NOS)","Mixed cytopenia and proliferation (WBC $ \\ge 13 \\times 10^9/L $ or Plt $ \\ge 450 \\times 10^9/L $), blasts \< 20%, lacking criteria for defined subtypes. Includes the provisional subentity MDS/MPN with i(17q) 51-56."

### V. Premalignant Clonal Cytopenias and Myelodysplastic Syndromes (MDS)

MDS classification now heavily relies on the genetic profile of the disease, completely eliminating the 2016 "MDS-unclassifiable" and "MDS with ring sideroblasts" categories 57, 58\.  
Disease Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
Clonal Cytopenia of Undetermined Significance (CCUS),"Persistent unexplained cytopenia associated with a somatic myeloid driver mutation (VAF $ \\ge 2\\% $), but specifically lacking the BM morphologic dysplasia or blast excess required for MDS 59, 60."  
MDS with mutated SF3B1 (MDS-SF3B1),"Cytopenia, \< 5% BM blasts, and an SF3B1 mutation ($ \\ge 10\\% $ VAF), explicitly lacking multi-hit TP53 or RUNX1 mutations. Ring sideroblasts are typically present but no longer strictly required 57, 58, 61."  
MDS with del(5q),"Cytopenia (thrombocytosis allowed), \< 5% BM blasts, and isolated del(5q) with up to 1 additional abnormality (excluding chromosome 7 aberrations or multi-hit TP53) 58, 62."  
MDS with mutated TP53,"Cytopenia, 0–9% blasts, defined strictly by multi-hit TP53 alterations (two distinct mutations, or one mutation with 17p deletion, copy-neutral LOH, or \>50% VAF). Highly aggressive clinical course 63, 64."  
"MDS, NOS without dysplasia","Cytopenia, \< 5% BM blasts, lacking definitive morphologic dysplasia, but presenting with MDS-defining cytogenetics like \-7/del(7q) or a complex karyotype 62."  
"MDS, NOS with single lineage dysplasia","Cytopenia, \< 5% BM blasts, morphologic dysplasia restricted to 1 lineage, lacking defining genetic categories 62, 65."  
"MDS, NOS with multilineage dysplasia","Cytopenia, \< 5% BM blasts, morphologic dysplasia in $ \\ge 2 $ lineages, lacking defining genetic categories 65, 66."  
MDS with excess blasts (MDS-EB),"Cytopenia with 5%–9% blasts in the BM or 2%–9% blasts in the PB. Any underlying single/multilineage dysplasia is superseded by blast count 65, 66."  
MDS/Acute Myeloid Leukemia (MDS/AML),"A biological continuum category capturing cytopenic patients with 10%–19% blasts in the blood or BM. Stratified by genetics into: MDS/AML with mutated TP53, MDS/AML with MR-gene mutations, MDS/AML with MR-cytogenetics, or MDS/AML, NOS 63, 66-68."

### VI. Pediatric and/or Germline Mutation-Associated Disorders

Germline predisposition serves as both a primary disease driver in pediatric cases and an obligatory diagnostic qualifier to be appended to adult MDS/AML phenotypes 69-71.  
Disease Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
Juvenile Myelomonocytic Leukemia (JMML),"PB monocytes $ \\ge 1 \\times 10^9/L $, splenomegaly, blasts \< 20%, and constitutive activation of the RAS pathway via somatic or germline mutations in PTPN11, KRAS, NRAS, NF1, or CBL 72-75."  
JMML-like Neoplasms,"Phenotypic mimics of JMML that definitively lack a RAS-pathway mutation, occasionally driven by rare fusions like ALK, ROS1, or FIP1L1::RARA 76-78."  
Noonan Syndrome–Associated MPD,"A transient, polyclonal myeloproliferative disorder in infants with syndromic Noonan germline mutations (PTPN11, KRAS, NRAS, RIT1) without somatic acquisition 78, 79."  
Refractory Cytopenia of Childhood (RCC),"Persistent cytopenia in a child accompanied by patchy, marked BM hypocellularity and significant dysplasia; blasts \< 5% BM. Often a precursor to monosomy 7 80-82."  
Hematologic neoplasms with germline predisposition,"Integrated as a diagnostic qualifier for overarching malignancies, mapped to three groups: (1) Without constitutional disorder (CEBPA, DDX41, TP53); (2) With constitutional platelet disorder (RUNX1, ANKRD26, ETV6); (3) With multiple organ system disorders (GATA2, SAMD9/SAMD9L, Fanconi Anemia, Down syndrome) 70, 83-87."

### VII. Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML)

The 2022 ICC significantly shifts AML classification towards a strictly genetic hierarchy. The historically monolithic 20% blast threshold has been lowered to **$ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts** for entities defined by highly specific recurring genetic anomalies, recognizing that their presence dictates clinical acute leukemia behavior regardless of blast count 88-90.  
Disease Entity,Diagnostic Definition & Genetic Criteria  
Acute Promyelocytic Leukemia (APL) with PML::RARA,Defined by t(15;17)(q24.1;q21.2) / PML::RARA. Requires $ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts/promyelocytes 91\.  
APL with other RARA rearrangements,"Fusions involving RARA with partner genes like NPM1, ZBTB16, IRF2BP2, STAT5B, etc. Requires $ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts 91, 92."  
AML with RUNX1::RUNX1T1,Defined by t(8;21)(q22;q22.1). Requires $ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts 91\.  
AML with CBFB::MYH11,Defined by inv(16)(p13.1q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22). Requires $ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts 91\.  
AML with MLLT3::KMT2A,Defined by t(9;11)(p21.3;q23.3). Requires $ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts 91\.  
AML with other KMT2A rearrangements,"Involves 11q23.3 translocations with partners such as AFF1, AFDN, MLLT10, TET1, ELL, MLLT1. Requires $ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts 91, 93."  
AML with DEK::NUP214,Defined by t(6;9)(p22.3;q34.1). Requires $ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts 91\.  
AML with GATA2; MECOM(EVI1),Defined by inv(3)(q21.3q26.2) or t(3;3)(q21.3;q26.2). Requires $ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts 91\.  
AML with other MECOM rearrangements,"Rare translocations involving 3q26.2. Requires $ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts 68, 93."  
AML with other rare recurring translocations,Specific structural abnormalities mapped by the ICC. Requires $ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts 68\.  
AML with mutated NPM1,Defined by somatic mutations in the NPM1 gene. Requires $ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts 68\.  
AML with in-frame bZIP CEBPA mutations,"Defined strictly by in-frame mutations mapping to the basic leucine zipper (bZIP) domain of CEBPA (biallelic status no longer required). Requires $ \\ge 10\\% $ blasts 68, 94."  
AML with mutated TP53,"Aggressive phenotype requiring $ \\ge 20\\% $ blasts (or fulfilling criteria for pure erythroid leukemia). Defined by any pathogenic TP53 mutation \>10% VAF 63, 68, 89."  
AML with BCR::ABL1,Defined by t(9;22)(q34.1;q11.2). Requires $ \\ge 20\\% $ blasts to differentiate from CML-AP 68\.  
AML with myelodysplasia-related gene mutations,"Replaces the prior ""AML-MRC"" category. Defined by $ \\ge 20\\% $ blasts and mutations in ASXL1, BCOR, EZH2, RUNX1, SF3B1, SRSF2, STAG2, U2AF1, or ZRSR2 68, 89."  
AML with myelodysplasia-related cytogenetic abnormalities,"Defined by $ \\ge 20\\% $ blasts and specific karyotypic lesions (e.g., complex karyotype, \-7/del(7q), del(5q), i(17q)) lacking TP53 mutations or specific AML drivers 68, 92."  
"AML, Not Otherwise Specified (NOS)","Defined by $ \\ge 20\\% $ blasts, lacking any of the defining genetic abnormalities, fusions, or myelodysplasia-related drivers listed above 92, 95."  
Myeloid Sarcoma,"Extramedullary proliferation of myeloid blasts obliterating tissue architecture, maintaining its status as a distinct tissue presentation 92."
