# Internal Communications Guide

This skill helps write professional internal communications.

## When to Use This Skill

- 3P updates (Progress, Plans, Problems)
- Company newsletters
- FAQ responses
- Status reports
- Leadership updates
- Project updates
- Incident reports

## Communication Types

### 1. Status Reports

**Structure:**
- What was accomplished this week
- What was worked on but not completed
- Planned for next week
- Blockers or concerns

**Tone:** Concise, factual, action-oriented

### 2. 3P Updates (Progress/Plans/Problems)

**Structure:**
- **Progress**: What was completed
- **Plans**: What's planned for next period
- **Problems**: Issues, blockers, concerns

**Tone:** Brief, transparent, solution-focused

### 3. Leadership Updates

**Structure:**
- Executive summary (1-2 sentences)
- Key wins/highlights
- Challenges and responses
- Strategic initiatives status
- Looking ahead

**Tone:** High-level, strategic, confident

### 4. Project Updates

**Structure:**
- Project name and status (Green/Yellow/Red)
- Milestones completed
- Upcoming milestones
- Risks and mitigation
- Resource needs

**Tone:** Clear, organized, forward-looking

### 5. Incident Reports

**Structure:**
- What happened (brief)
- Impact
- Root cause
- Resolution
- Prevention measures
- Timeline

**Tone:** Factual, transparent, accountable

### 6. Newsletters

**Structure:**
- Company news
- Team highlights
- Upcoming events
- Recognition/cross-functional

**Tone:** Engaging, positive, inclusive

### 7. FAQs

**Structure:**
- Question (bold)
- Answer (concise)

**Tone:** Clear, helpful, consistent

## Best Practices

1. **Know your audience** - Who is receiving this?
2. **Lead with the key point** - Most important info first
3. **Be concise** - Get to the point quickly
4. **Use structure** - Bullet points, headers for readability
5. **Include actionable next steps** - What should reader do?
6. **Set expectations** - Timeline, follow-up details

## Format Guidelines

- Subject line: Clear, descriptive
- Length: As short as possible while covering essentials
- Action items: Bold or clearly marked
- Questions: Address upfront
